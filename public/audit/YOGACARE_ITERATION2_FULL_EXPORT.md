# SECTION E — DIFFERENTIATION SELF-AUDIT

### 1. Does the homepage visually show adaptation, or just state it?
**Mostly states it.** The copy says "adapts" and "built around your current needs." The signal bar shows "Balance • Support • Restore" which hints at categorization. But there is no dynamic or visual proof of adaptation on the homepage itself. The TailoringSnapshot component (which IS visual proof) only appears on the Plan page after onboarding. The homepage relies entirely on copy.

### 2. Does it feel distinct from Down Dog?
**Partially.** The dark hero with gold accents, the SVG wave illustration, and the brand positioning ("Adaptive Yoga for Modern Bodies") create a different tone — more premium-clinical vs. Down Dog's friendly-accessible. However, the card-based layout below the fold is generic enough to feel interchangeable with many wellness apps. The Three Pillars concept is distinctive but underexplored visually.

### 3. Does the hero feel empty?
**No, but it feels quiet.** The AdaptiveWaveHero SVG provides subtle motion and texture. The typography is strong and the gold CTA is prominent. However, at 12% opacity the waves are barely visible. The hero could support more visual confidence — a more pronounced illustration or a motion element would strengthen it.

### 4. Does it feel premium or sparse?
**Premium-leaning.** The dark hero, generous whitespace, Plus Jakarta Sans typography, gold accents, and card system with soft shadows create a high-end feel. The Settings and Library pages feel polished. Some areas (Complete page with hardcoded emerald, exercise data in Hebrew) undercut the premium impression.

### 5. Are there inconsistencies?
**Yes:**
- Exercise data (titles, cues, breathing, safety, reps, why text) is still largely **Hebrew** in the master data files. The i18n maps only translate UI chrome (conditions, red flags, equipment etc.), not the 30+ master exercises.
- "גב אמצעי" appears untranslated on the Questionnaire Step 2 (missing from PAIN_AREAS_EN).
- CompletePage uses `bg-emerald-100` and `text-emerald-600` — hardcoded colors not from design system.
- The hero CTA button uses inline bg colors (`bg-[hsl(var(--color-gold))]`) rather than the semantic `bg-accent` class.
- HeroSection gradient uses inline `hsl(215,50%,11%)` values instead of CSS variables.

### 6. Are there placeholder visuals?
**Yes:**
- `PageIllustration` component renders abstract SVG icons (shield, list, settings, sliders, check, calendar) — they are decorative placeholders, not content-rich illustrations.
- Exercise animations use `ExerciseVideoAnimation` which likely falls back to placeholder SVG figures when no video is available.
- The Yael photo uses a real image (`/assets/yael/yoga.jpg`) which renders correctly.

# SECTION F — AVATAR CONCEPT FOR VIDEO PRODUCTION

## YogaCare Avatar Character Specification

### Demographics & Appearance
- **Gender:** Female-presenting (inclusive, not hyper-gendered)
- **Age range:** Late 30s to early 40s
- **Body type:** Athletic-natural, realistic proportions — not model-thin, not bodybuilder. Healthy, grounded physique that represents someone who practices regularly.
- **Ethnicity:** Ethnicity-neutral / global look — warm medium skin tone, features that don't strongly signal any single ethnicity. Think Mediterranean-South Asian blend.
- **Hair:** Dark brown, shoulder-length, pulled back in a low relaxed bun or loose ponytail. No flyaways, no elaborate styling. Clean and practical.
- **Facial expression:** Calm, focused, neutral-pleasant. Slight closed-lip softness. Eyes soft or gently closed during breath work, open and steady during movement.

### Clothing
- **Top:** Fitted long-sleeve performance top in deep ink navy (#0E1A2B) with subtle gold accent stitching along the neckline or sleeve cuff
- **Bottom:** High-waisted cropped leggings in warm charcoal (#2A2A2A) or deep sage (#4A6B64)
- **Feet:** Barefoot
- **No logos, no text, no branded gear**
- Fabric should look matte, premium athleisure — think Lululemon or Alo Yoga quality, not gym-generic

### Studio Environment
- **Setting:** Minimal premium yoga studio
- **Floor:** Light warm wood (birch or maple tone, approximately #E8D5B5)
- **Background:** Soft off-white to warm cream wall (#F6F5F1 — matching brand background)
- **No furniture except:** One rolled yoga mat in sage green beside the character (not being used)
- **No props visible during exercises unless specified (blocks, strap)**
- **Space:** Generous negative space around character — character takes up ~60% of frame height

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Soft diffused top-front lighting, slightly warm (3200–3800K feel)
- **Fill:** Gentle ambient from left
- **No harsh shadows, no dramatic rim lights**
- **Overall mood:** Warm, calm, high-end wellness studio at golden hour
- **No visible light sources, windows show soft cream/white**

### Quality Standards
- Not hyper-realistic (avoid uncanny valley)
- Not cartoonish
- Aim for: high-end 3D render or premium motion capture quality
- Should feel like a real person in a real studio, just slightly idealized
- Must NOT resemble any specific real person or celebrity
